## Activity

> Calculate how many years and months ahead from now Margie’s diary entry is.

**Ans:**  Margie’s diary entry is dated **17 May 2157**.  
From now, it is about **131 years and several months ahead**.

## I. Answer these questions in a few words or a couple of sentences each.

### 1. How old are Margie and Tommy?

**Ans:** Margie is **eleven years old** and Tommy is **thirteen years old**.

### 2. What did Margie write in her diary?

**Ans:** Margie wrote in her diary that **Tommy had found a real book**.

### 3. Had Margie ever seen a book before?

**Ans:** No, Margie had **never seen a real printed book** before.

### 4. What things about the book did she find strange?

**Ans:** She found it strange that the words **did not move**, and that the book had to be **read again from the beginning** once finished.

### 5. What do you think a telebook is?

**Ans:** A telebook is an **electronic book** that is read on a screen.

### 6. Where was Margie’s school? Did she have any classmates?

**Ans:** Margie’s school was **in her house**, next to her bedroom.  
She **did not have classmates**.

### 7. What subjects did Margie and Tommy learn?

**Ans:** They learned subjects like **History, Geography, and Arithmetic**.

## II. Answer the following with reference to the story.

### 1. “I wouldn’t throw it away.”

#### (i) Who says these words?

**Ans:** **Tommy** says these words.

#### (ii) What does ‘it’ refer to?

**Ans:** ‘It’ refers to the **real book**.

#### (iii) What is it being compared with by the speaker?

**Ans:** It is being compared with **television screens and electronic books**.

### 2. “Sure they had a teacher, but it wasn’t a regular teacher. It was a man.”

#### (i) Who does ‘they’ refer to?

**Ans:** ‘They’ refers to **children from the old schools**.

#### (ii) What does ‘regular’ mean here?

**Ans:** ‘Regular’ means **not mechanical**.

#### (iii) What is it contrasted with?

**Ans:** It is contrasted with a **mechanical teacher**.

## III. Answer each of these questions in a short paragraph (about 30 words).

### 1. What kind of teachers did Margie and Tommy have?

**Ans:** Tommy and Margie were taught by **mechanical teachers**. These instructors used screens to deliver courses, assign assignments, and tests automatically.

### 2. Why did Margie’s mother send for the County Inspector?

**Ans:** Because of Margie's poor performance in **Geography** , her mother believed that the mechanical teacher was malfunctioning.

### 3. What did he do?

**Ans:** The County Inspector **checked and adjusted** the mechanical teacher and slowed down the Geography lessons.

### 4. Why was Margie doing badly in Geography? What did the County Inspector do to help her?

**Ans:** The lessons were too fast for Margie. The Inspector **reset the teacher** to suit her age level.

### 5. What had once happened to Tommy’s teacher?

**Ans:** Tommy’s teacher **blanked out completely** and had to be taken away for repairs.

### 6. Did Margie have regular days and hours for school? If so, why?

**Ans:** Yes, Margie had fixed school hours because her mother believed that **regular learning habits** helped children learn better.

### 7. How does Tommy describe the old kind of school?

**Ans:** Tommy says that old schools had **human teachers**, children learned together, and it was more fun than studying alone.

## IV. Answer each of these questions in two or three paragraphs (100–150 words).

### 1. What are the main features of the mechanical teachers and the schoolrooms that Margie and Tommy have in the story?

**Ans:** The mechanical teachers were machines that taught lessons on a screen. They gave homework, tests, and marks automatically. Each child studied alone at home, without classmates. The schoolroom was a small room with a screen and slots for homework. The teaching speed could be adjusted, but learning was lonely and boring.

### 2. Why did Margie hate school? Why did she think the old kind of school must have been fun?

**Ans:** Margie hated school because it was boring, lonely, and controlled by machines. She disliked doing homework daily and taking tests without friends. She thought old schools were fun because children learned together, had human teachers, and shared happiness and experiences. She imagined laughter, friendship, and enjoyment in old classrooms.
